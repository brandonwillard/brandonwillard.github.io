In this document we investigate models for COVID–19.

Introduction

We start with a TSIR model defined as follows:

\[\begin{equation} \begin{gathered} I_t = r_t I_{t-1} \frac{S_{t-1}}{N_t} \epsilon_t \quad \epsilon_t \sim \operatorname{Pois}\left( 1 \right) \\ S_t = S_{t-1} - I_t \\ r_t = \frac{1}{1 + \exp\left( -\eta_t \right)} \\ \eta_t = \phi \eta_{t-1} + \omega_t, \quad \omega_t \sim \operatorname{N}\left( 0, 1 \right) \end{gathered} \end{equation}\]

where \(S_t\) and \(I_t\) are susceptible and infected numbers, respectively, and \(r_t\) is a transmission rate. The total population, \(N\), over the observation period is assumed to obey \(S_t + I_t = N\) at least approximately.

Furthermore, \(I_t\) is never directly observed, so, as in we let \(I_t = \rho_t C_t\), where \(\rho_t \ge 1\) is a reporting rate and \(\operatorname{E}\left[ \rho_t \right] = \rho\).
